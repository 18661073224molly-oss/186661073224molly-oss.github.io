Margherita Pizza

Dish Introduction

Pizza (Italian: Pizza) is a typical Italian dish made from fermented round bread with tomato sauce, cheese and other toppings and baked in the oven. Cheese is usually mozzarella cheese or a mixture of several cheeses...

In 1889, King Umberto I and Queen Margherita of Italy visited Naples. Local chef Raffaele Esposito created a pizza named after the Queen, using tomatoes, mozzarella cheese and basil to show the colors of the Italian flag. The Queen loved it very much, and this pizza became famous.

Ingredients

Flour
150g
Water
90g
Yeast
2g
Salt
3g
Cherry tomatoes
100g
Mozzarella cheese
120g
Olive oil
10g
Parsley leaves
2 pieces

Practice Introduction

Dough preparation

Mix the flour, natural yeast, salt and water in the exact proportions. Then knead the dough by hand using techniques such as kneading and stretching to make the dough surface smooth and elastic.

Dough fermentation

Place the kneaded dough in a fermentation box at a room temperature of 26¬∞C and humidity of 75%-80%, and ferment for 8-12 hours. This way, the fermented dough will increase by 50% in size, and it can quickly return to its original shape when pressed.

Cake bottom molding

Use fermented dough balls to form a rolling pin, rolling gently into a disk shape with your fingers. Then throw the dough into the air to rotate it and stretch it to a diameter of about 30-35 cm and a slightly thick edge.

Spread and add

Spread tomato sauce evenly on the base of the pie, spread the surface fully with mozzarella cheese, sprinkle with fresh basil leaves, season well with a little extra virgin olive oil.

Baking process

Using a traditional wood-burning oven, the temperature is as high as 400-450¬∞C, baking for 90 seconds to 2 minutes, so that the edges of the cake are quickly scorched, and the interior is soft and elastic.
